## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**The Core Problem:**  
We have approximately **400,000+ dormant contacts** (from a total quiz list of 2.3M, with ~500K identified as inactive). These people gave us permission once. We stopped earning it. This campaign is about re-earning the right to their attention before we try to sell them anything.

**The Strategy:**  
A two-phase reactivation campaign:
1. **Phase 1 (Re-Permission):** Free ethical gift + intelligence-gathering survey
2. **Phase 2 (Segmented Selling):** Type-specific offers based on survey data

**Revenue Expectations:**  
This is a LONG GAME. Phase 1 generates zero revenue. Phase 2 will generate $40K-$120K over 90 days if executed well, but the real value is reactivating 5,000-20,000 engaged contacts for ongoing relationship.

**This is not a side project. This is the priority.**

## THE REALITY CHECK: CONVERSION MATH

Let's be honest about what reactivation campaigns achieve with dormant lists:

### Industry-Standard Metrics for Cold Reactivation:
- **Email Open Rate:** 5-15% (dormant lists are COLD)
- **Click Rate:** 1-3% (of total sent, not just openers)
- **Free Gift Claim Rate:** 2-8% (depends on perceived value)
- **Survey Completion Rate:** 50-70% of gift claimers
- **Gift Claimer → Paid Conversion:** 0.5-2% (over 90 days)

### What This Means for 400,000 Contacts:

**Phase 1: Ethical Gift Campaign**
- 400,000 emails sent
- 20,000-60,000 opens (5-15%)
- 4,000-12,000 clicks (1-3%)
- 8,000-32,000 gift claims (2-8% claim rate)
- 4,000-22,400 survey completions (50-70% of claimers)

**Phase 2: Conversion to Paid (90 days post-gift)**
- 40-640 buyers from gift recipients (0.5-2% conversion)
- Average order value: $69 (Pleasure Playbook) to $150 (blended with mini-courses)
- **Revenue Range: $2,760 - $96,000**

**Conservative Planning Number: $40,000 revenue in first 90 days**  
**Optimistic But Realistic: $80,000-$120,000**

### The Real Win:
This isn't about immediate revenue. It's about:
1. **Reactivating 5,000-20,000 engaged contacts** who opt back in
2. **Gathering intelligence** on what they want and how they want to learn
3. **Cleaning the list** (removing hard bounces, unengaged contacts)
4. **Re-earning permission** to market to them long-term

## PHASE 1: EMAIL SEQUENCE

### Email 1: The Invitation (Day 1)

**Subject Lines (A/B test):**
- "The techniques we never taught you..."
- "Your blueprint was just the beginning"
- "[FirstName], remember your Erotic Blueprint?"

**Preview Text:** "Unlock 3 advanced techniques designed for your type (free training inside)"

**Email Structure:**
- **Open:** "You took the quiz. You discovered your Erotic Blueprint Type. But we never gave you the techniques..."
- **The Gap:** "Knowing your type is like having a map. But you still need directions."
- **The Gift:** "That's why I want to give you our Blueprintified Sex Techniques training - 3 videos that show you exactly how to USE your blueprint in real, intimate moments."
- **The Ask:** "But first, I need to know: are you still interested? And if so, what do you most want to transform?"
- **CTA:** "Click here to claim your free training (and tell us what you need)"
- **No deadline, no pressure.** Just: are you still with us?

**Psychological Trigger:** Curiosity + Incompletion + Gift reciprocity

---

### Email 2: The Reminder (Day 4)

**Send to:** Non-clickers from Email 1

**Subject Lines:**
- "Still holding your free training..."
- "Did you miss this?"
- "Your [BlueprintType] techniques are waiting"

**Email Structure:**
- **Softer open:** "I sent you something a few days ago and wanted to make sure you saw it..."
- **Restate the gift:** 3-part video training, completely free
- **Social proof:** "Over [X] people have already claimed theirs"
- **Remove friction:** "Takes 60 seconds to unlock - just answer one question"
- **CTA:** "Get your free training here"

**Psychological Trigger:** FOMO (others are claiming it) + Low friction

---

### Email 3: The Last Call (Day 8)

**Send to:** Non-clickers from Emails 1 & 2

**Subject Lines:**
- "Last chance for your free training"
- "We're closing access to this gift"
- "Final reminder: Your blueprint techniques"

**Email Structure:**
- **Direct:** "This is the last email about this. We're closing access to the Blueprintified Techniques training after this week."
- **Why?** "We want to focus our energy on people who are ready to engage. If that's you, grab this now."
- **The gift:** One more description of what they get
- **Final CTA:** "Yes, I want the free training"
- **The exit:** "If this isn't for you right now, no problem - just click here to update your preferences"

**Psychological Trigger:** Deadline + Permission to leave (paradoxically increases action)

---

### Email 4: The Clean Break (Day 12)

**Send to:** Non-clickers from Emails 1-3

**Subject Line:** "Should we break up?"

**Email Structure:**
- **Honest open:** "I've sent you 3 emails about free training. You haven't clicked. That tells me something."
- **The question:** "Are we still a good fit? Are you still interested in improving your intimate life through The Blueprints?"
- **Two paths:**
  - **Path A (Yes):** "Click here to grab your free training and stay on our active list"
  - **Path B (No):** "Click here to unsubscribe - no hard feelings"
- **The truth:** "We only want to talk to people who want to hear from us."

**Psychological Trigger:** Authenticity + Forced choice + Reverse psychology

- **Tag:** `V-Reactivated-Nurture` (they're warm, just not ready yet)

**The Long Game:**  
These people re-engaged. They're no longer dormant. They may convert in 6-12 months. Keep the relationship alive.

## RISKS & MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Low Gift Claim Rate (<2%)

**Why it might happen:**
- Free gift isn't compelling enough
- Survey feels like too much friction
- Emails not reaching inbox (deliverability issue)

**Mitigation:**
- A/B test subject lines aggressively
- Keep survey SHORT (2 required questions max)
- Monitor inbox placement daily, adjust if needed
- If claim rate is low by Day 3, send emergency "simplified" email with lower barrier

---

### Risk 2: High Unsubscribe Rate (>1%)

**Why it might happen:**
- List is colder than we thought
- Messaging feels salesy (even though it's a gift)
- People forgot they ever signed up

**Mitigation:**
- Lead with value, not pitch (Phase 1 is 100% give, 0% sell)
- Make unsubscribe link easy to find (reduces spam complaints)
- Include "update preferences" option (lets people reduce email frequency instead of full unsub)
- ACCEPT that some attrition is healthy (removes dead weight)

---

### Risk 3: Survey Data is Useless (People Don't Answer Honestly or Questions Don't Discriminate)

**Why it might happen:**
- Questions too vague or confusing
- People rush through to get free gift
- Answer options don't match what they actually want

**Mitigation:**
- Test survey with small group before launch (get feedback on clarity)
- Make questions genuinely useful (not just data for data's sake)
- Review first 500 responses on Day 2 - if data is garbage, adjust questions for Email 2 recipients

---

### Risk 4: Phase 2 Conversion Lower Than Projected

**Why it might happen:**
- Gift recipients still not ready to buy
- Offer/price mismatch
- Poor sales copy

**Mitigation:**
- Set conservative expectations (we already did this)
- Have backup offers ready (lower price point, payment plan, different product)
- Monitor daily conversion and adjust if needed (extend deadline, add bonus, etc.)
- Remember: even "failed" Phase 2 still gives us reactivated list for long-term nurture

---

### Risk 5: Technical Failure (Survey Doesn't Tag, Emails Don't Send, Videos Don't Play)

**Why it might happen:**
- Webhook breaks
- Ontraport glitch
- Video hosting fails

**Mitigation:**
- Test EVERYTHING Day -3 (full user journey, multiple times)
- Have manual backup plan (can manually tag survey completers if webhook fails)
- Use reliable hosting (Vimeo Pro, not free YouTube)
- Have tech support on standby Day 1-3 (highest volume, highest risk)
